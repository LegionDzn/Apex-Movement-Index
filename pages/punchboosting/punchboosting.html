Punchboosting

AKA. Melee Boosting

Basic Punchboost

Punchboosting is one of the most versatile and widely applicable movement techs in Apex right now. A punch boost can be performed by crouching, holding the BACKWARD input, and meleeing any surface higher than the one you are standing on even if it's by a very small amount. If performed correctly, you should be boosted BACKWARD.

You can also punch boost without crouching. To do this you must be standing then melee the surface and input a JUMP just after the melee connects with the surface.

You can also punchboost off certain legend abilities such as: Loba's Black Market, Wattson's Generator, Caustic Traps, Revanant's totem, Lifeline's carepack and heal drone, Mirage decoys, Crypto's drone, and Gibby's bubble.

Slope Punchboosting

To punch boost, you must melee a surface that is higher than the one you are on. When on a slope, the nature of the surface allows you to punchboost by just meleeing the ground upslope even if the slope is very shallow. If this slope is large enough, you'll be able to chain these punchboosts to get an extremely large amount of momentum, which is what I do in the video.

If you do not realize it already, this is basically a better version of sliding down slopes and can be used in the same way. The sope doesn't have to be large, as long as your melee can connect with the ground while looking upslope.

Punchboost Tapstrafe

After exiting a punchboost your momentum can be redirected with a tapstrafe or momentumshift, altohugh the latter notabliy harder.

I find it easier and more versatile to tapstrafe out of a standing punchboost than a sliding one due to the fact that once jump following the melee you can tapstrafe immidedetly, insted of having to jump out of a slide with the former.
