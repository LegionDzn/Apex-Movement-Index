Momentum Shifting

W+A+S+D redirect

A momentum shift is a blanket term that encompasses anything which utilizes lurch to shift your momentum.
The momentum shift highlighted in this article is a 180-degree momentum shift, allowing you to turn 180° without moving your camera. This momentum shift is most useful for dodging in close-range gunfights and backsliding for pathfinder grapples.


This momentum shift can be performed by inputting 4 movement keys, these inputs are most effective when pressed individually, this is difficult since this tech takes advantage of lurch, which only lasts 400ms or 4/10ths a second after you jump. If performed correctly you should be able to hold before you land into a slide. But note that this momentum shift also has a range of applications even if you don't slide.

These inputs vary depending on the direction in which you want to travel for example I will use a wasd redirect.
The inputs go as follows:

1. Let go of the direction key in which you are traveling while you input a jump. (w)
2. Input a direction key perpendicular to the way you are traveling. (a)
3. Input a direction key opposite of the direction you were initially traveling. (s)
4. Input a direction key opposite of the perpendicular direction input you previously pressed. (d)

You can also adjust how long you hold the final input key (d in this example) for increased control of your final position.

The 4 momentum shifts grow increasingly weak with speed so to effectively utilize this at a high velocity, we have to use a modified momentum shift called a ras strafe.

Tapstrafe + Forward / Backward Momentum Shift

You can tapstrafe out of a forward / backward redirect to allow yourself to land with your momentum going forward.

to perform this simply forward / backward momentum shift and hold your last directional input while scrolling FORWARD. Timing the tap strafe is somewhat difficult due to the fact that it has to be after the redirect, but not too soon after else you will lose all your momentum. All within the 500ms lurch window.
