Supergliding with Keyboard Binds

A superglide can be performed by mantling onto a surface, then while mantling holding a direction key (or two such as FORWARD + LEFT) except BACKWARD and inputting a DUCK and JUMP at the same time at the apex of a mantel animation. This, if performed correctly, will result in your character accelerating to 550 velocity nearly instantaneously in the direction of the previously held directional input. The timing for supergliding has a very tight, 1-3 frame window near the apex of the mantle animation, so 50ms at 60fps or 20.7ms at 144fps. DUCK must be inputted one frame before JUMP and must both be inputted within this window to successfully execute a superglide.
In this video I demonstrate supergliding with a JUMP being a keyboard bind, to aid in inputting a DUCK and JUMP withing the window, it's recommended that you bind either C, V, or B to crouch allowing you to press DUCK and JUMP with one motion of the thumb.
If you have difficulty supergliding or want to superglide with more consistency, I recommend using a program called RTSS to cap your framerate. I and others have noted that it's measurably easier to superglide with this program capping your fps. For more information, check out (Optimizing for Latency)

Directional Supergliding

Here is a video and a list of all possible directional superglides on MnK.
The list is in order in which I perform them in the video.

• (A+S+D) LEFT + BACKWARD + RIGHT (Psudo BACKWARD superglide, BACKWARD and RIGHT must be inputted slightly after the superglide. Note that this utilize lurch.)

• (A) LEFT

• (A+W) LEFT + FORWARDS

• (W) FORWARDS

• (D+W) RIGHT + FORWARDS

• (D) RIGHT

• (D+S+A) RIGHT + BACKWARD + LEFT (Psudo BACKWARD superglide, BACKWARD and LEFT must be inputted slightly after the superglide. Note that this utilize lurch.)
