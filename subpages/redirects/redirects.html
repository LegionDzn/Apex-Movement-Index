Momentum Shifting

Basic Momentum Shift (180 degrees)

This video shows the most basic of Momentum Shifts
A momentum shift is tech which allows you to shift your momentum in roughly either 90 or 180 degrees. This is most useful in close range gunfights, but also has other applications in Grapples and Jumppads.
A momentum shift can be performed by inputting 4 movement keys, these inputs are most effective when pressed individually, this is difficult since this tech takes advantage of lurch, which only lasts 1/2 second after you jump. If performed correctly you can hold crouch after or while you perform this tech to initiate a slide after you land.
These inputs vary depending on the direction in which you want to travel for example I will use a wasd redirect.
The inputs go as follows:

1. Let go of the direction key in which you are traveling while you input a jump. (w)
2. Input a direction key perpendicular to the way you are traveling. (a)
3. Input a direction key opposite of the direction you were initially traveling. (s)
4. Input a direction key opposite of the perpendicular direction input you previously pressed. (d)

You can also adjust how long you hold the final input key (d in this example) for increased control of your final position.

Forward / Backward Momentum Shift (180 degrees)

The video shows the most common uses for momentum shifts in the forward / backward direction.
Here is a list of the inputs used in the video, they are in order of the individual momentum shits.

1st. w > dsa
2nd. w > asd
3rd. s > dwa
4th. s > awd

Left / Right Momentum Shift (180 degrees)

The video shows the most common uses for momentum shifts in the left / right direction.
Here is a list of the inputs used in the video, they are in order of the individual momentum shits.

1st. d > saw
2nd. d > was
3rd. a > wds
4th. a > sdw
