Tapstrafing

Changing Direction With Tap Strafe

A tap strafe is an act of stacking lurch to allow yourself to turn as sharply as you can move your mouse while preserving a very high amount of your momentum. To tapstrafe simply spam the FORWARD (Usually by scrolling) input while looking in the direction you want to go and holding LEFT or RIGHT depending on the direction you are turning. Note that you don't have to hold a directional key (eg. LEFT or RIGHT), but you won't turn as sharply if you don't.
When tapstrafing while holding LEFT or RIGHT, your direction will be offset by 45 degrees when you finish your tapstrafe due to you lurching diagonally to tapstrafe. In simpler terms, when you perform 180-degree tapstrafe when holding either LEFT or RIGHT, you'll only need to turn to 135-degrees (180-45) to do a full 180. You can notice this effect by tapstrafing without moving your camera.
Tapstrafing is arguably the most useful complex movement tech in the apex right now, from having synergies with nearly every movement ability in the game to being useful anywhere from gunfights to running across the map, tapstrafing has you covered.
Tapstrafing works by abusing lurch thereby must be performed when your lurch is charged (aka 1/2 seconds after a jump). By stacking these lurches by spam inputting FORWARD, we can allow ourselves to turn extremely sharply.
