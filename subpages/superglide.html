Supergliding with Keyboard Binds

Supergliding with Keyboard Binds

A superglide can be performed by mantling onto a surface, then while mantling holding a direction key (or two such as FORWARD + LEFT) except BACKWARDS and inputting a DUCK and JUMP at the same time at the apex of a mantel animation. This, if performed correctly, will result in your character accelerating to 550 velocity nearly instantaneously in the direction of the previously held directional input. The timing for supergliding has a very tight, 1-3 frame window near the apex of the mantle animation, so 50ms at 60fps or 20.7ms at 144fps. DUCK must be inputted one frame before JUMP and must both be inputted within this window to successfully execute a superglide.
In this video I demonstrate supergliding with a JUMP being a keyboard bind, to aid in inputting a DUCK and JUMP withing the window, it's recommended that you bind either C, V, or B to crouch allowing you to press DUCK and JUMP with one motion of the thumb.

Directional Supergliding

Directional Superglides

Here is a video and a list of all possible directional superglides on MnK.
The list is in order in which I perform them in the video.

• (A+S+D) LEFT + BACKWARDS + RIGHT (Psudo backwards superglide, BACKWARDS and RIGHT must be inputted slightly after the superglide. Note that this utilize lurch.)
• (A) LEFT
• (A+W) LEFT + FORWARDS
• (W) FORWARDS
• (D+W) RIGHT + FORWARDS
• (D) RIGHT
• (D+S+A) RIGHT + BACKWARDS + LEFT (Psudo backwards superglide, BACKWARDS and LEFT must be inputted slightly after the superglide. Note that this utilize lurch.)

Superglide Tapstrafing

Tapstrafing out of a Superglide

Supergliding, making use of a jump, also charges your lurch. Meaning that you can tapstrafe after performing a superglide. With propper uitilization of this tech directional superglides become somewhat obsolete.
